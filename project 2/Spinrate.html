Spin Rate: And why it is so important


The Basics of Spin Rate


Spin rate, is simply the rate of which the ball spins, this is measured by advanced camera's and computers who put the number into rpm's, rotation per minute. Now there are 4 types of spin, backspin, which is essential to a good fastball. Top spin, the spin put into a curveball to make it drop faster and late. Side spin, the ball will drop and the ball will move towards the spin. Gyro, bullet spin, or a spiral, like a football, a baseball thrown with Gyro spin won't move, it will only drop. Now you don't want a high spin rate on all pitches as a couple are specifically designed to not spin much, namely knuckleball's and splitters which due to their lack of spin they move randomly.


Leaders in spin rate


The current active leader is Ryan Helsley with an average four seam fastball spin of 2643 rpm. The leader for slider spin is Lucas Sims with an astonishing average spin of 3002 rpm. For curveball's, one of baseball's most iconic pitches, the spin rate leader is Ryan pressley with an average spin rate of 3290.
